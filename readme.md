“What if your robot could sense not just your face, but your voice too — without a microphone?” 🤖

While working on a conversational AI challenge, we learned something interesting — sometimes, the best way to detect speech isn’t through sound, but through sight.

As part of my internship at Neurocomputation Lab (NCL) we set out to solve this for ‘Luco Bot’, our AI-powered receptionist robot designed for human-robot interaction.

In our search for solutions, we explored MediaPipe Face Mesh, a lightweight face tracking framework capable of mapping 468 facial landmarks, including detailed points around the lips. It turned out to be a practical and effective tool for our use case — detecting which person is speaking in a group of multiple people standing in front of the robot, without relying on audio.

📌 What we built:

✔️ Integrated MediaPipe Face Mesh for real-time facial landmark tracking

✔️ Developed a lip-movement-based speaker detection system by measuring upper and lower lip distances

✔️ Applied dynamic calibration relative to each face’s size for reliable, adaptable performance in multi-user scenarios

📌 Takeaways:

▪️ AI works best when it combines technical precision with human-centered thinking

▪️ Subtle visual cues like lip movement can open new possibilities for natural, soundless interaction

▪️ Seems like robots are slowly learning the art of silent conversations. 👀
